In-situ testing in Boise provides direct measurement of soil and fill properties without disturbing the natural ground structure—an essential step for verifying compaction and bearing capacity across the Treasure Valley. Local geology, shaped by fluvial and lacustrine deposits from the Boise River and ancient Lake Idaho, often presents layered silts, sands, and gravels that demand field verification beyond laboratory estimates. Our field density test (sand cone method) delivers reliable in-place density data in accordance with ASTM D1556, ensuring compliance with project specifications and the International Building Code as adopted by the City of Boise.

These tests are critical for foundation subgrades, utility trench backfill, roadway base courses, and structural fill placement where loose or variable materials can compromise long-term performance. For a complete subsurface profile, in-situ density checks are frequently paired with soil boring and sampling to correlate compaction results with deeper stratigraphy. Whether you're developing a commercial pad near the Boise Airport or a residential subdivision in the foothills, accurate field testing reduces risk and streamlines earthwork acceptance.
